About Me

Hi, I’m Amanda, a software engineer based in Vancouver.

I’m naturally curious about how things work, especially the systems and logic operating behind the scenes of the web. I first discovered software development while working in the mechanical engineering and construction industry, where I saw how thoughtfully written code could automate hours of repetitive work. That experience changed the direction of my career and led me to pursue full-stack development.

Today, I primarily work on backend systems, building and maintaining the services and application logic that support real-world products. My work has involved integrating with external platforms, implementing access-control workflows, designing and consuming APIs, working with databases and complex queries, and tracing data through unfamiliar systems to diagnose production issues. I enjoy understanding how the different parts of an application communicate, identifying where behaviour or performance breaks down, and developing solutions that are reliable, maintainable, and grounded in how the product is actually used.

Outside of coding, movement is a major part of my life. You’ll often find me running, dancing, hiking, biking, climbing, or exploring the mountains around Vancouver. Whether I’m learning a new technical concept, refining choreography, or training toward a physical goal, I’m happiest when I’m challenged, improving, and creating something I’m proud of.
